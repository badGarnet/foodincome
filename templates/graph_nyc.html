distribution of restaurants and income in New York City, NY

NYC is different. Its local attractions, like Broadway greatly skews the placement of restaurants. Moreover, lots of new yorkers don't drive/take a car to travel across town. Instead they use subway. Therefore, the method of weighting income and household number by driving distance doesn't work so well in New York.

With that said, the accessible income map does reveal the west bank of Queens/Brooklyn can potentially attract relatively high revenue while the local income of those places are not purticularly high. The high number of restaurants in those neighborhoods (shown in the current restaurant density map) agrees with the finding from the accessible income map.
